## Scalping Strategy

## ① Double Doji Break (Trending Market)

**Market Context**
Price has a clear trend — most bars closing in the same direction, trend bars visibly taller and more decisive. Price makes a swing in the trend direction, then pulls back 40–60% of that swing. Pullback is diagonal and one-directional, not sideways chop.

**Setup Rules**
- 2 or more dojis appear at the end of the pullback
- Each doji max 3 pips — body nearly non-existent, open ≈ close
- Dojis must look visually compressed compared to surrounding trend bars
- Trend-side extremes (highs for long / lows for short) equal or max 1–2 pips apart
- Strong trend = 2 pip difference ok / Weak trend = 1 pip max
- 60-pip path ahead in trade direction must be clear — no clustering bars blocking it
- No round number (00 or 50 level) sitting directly between entry and target

**Signal Bar & Entry**
- Signal bar = the doji with the most extreme high (long) or lowest low (short)
- Entry bar = the bar that travels 1 pip beyond the signal bar extreme
- Fire market order the moment that 1-pip break occurs — no anticipation ever

**Stop & Target**
- Stop = 30 pips from entry (maximum)
- Target = 60 pips from entry (maximum)
- Ratio = always 1:2 minimum
- If market structure offers a tighter logical stop — use it and scale target down proportionally keeping 1:2
- Never widen the stop beyond 30 pips. Never take a target below 1:2

**Skip If Any of These**
- Pullback is horizontal or sideways, not diagonal
- Pullback bars are taller than trend bars
- Pullback retraces 100% of the prior move
- Dojis are not compressed vs surrounding bars
- Extremes more than 2 pips apart in a weak trend
- Clustering bars block the 60-pip path to target
- Round number sits between entry and target
- Pullback contains a higher low (long) or lower high (short) — countertrend structure forming

## ② First DD After Range/Consolidation Breakout

**Market Context**
Price has been consolidating sideways — a clear range or tight cluster with no trend. A decisive breakout occurs with tall, clean, one-directional bars breaking out of the range. This is a newborn trend — no prior trend required. After the breakout move, price pulls back toward the breakout zone.

**Setup Rules**
- 2 or more dojis form at the end of the pullback, in the area of the breakout zone
- Each doji max 3 pips — must look compressed vs the breakout bars
- Pullback is testing the breakout zone — former resistance becomes support (long) or vice versa (short)
- Trend-side extremes equal or max 1–2 pips apart
- 60-pip path ahead in the breakout direction must be clear — no resistance blocking it
- Original breakout bars were decisive — if they were weak, skip the entire setup

**Signal Bar & Entry**
- Signal bar = the doji with the most extreme high (long) or lowest low (short)
- Entry bar = the bar that travels 1 pip beyond the signal bar extreme
- Fire market order the moment that 1-pip break occurs — no anticipation ever

**Stop & Target**
- Stop = 30 pips from entry (maximum)
- Target = 60 pips from entry (maximum)
- Ratio = always 1:2 minimum
- If market structure offers a tighter logical stop — use it and scale target down proportionally keeping 1:2
- Never widen the stop beyond 30 pips. Never take a target below 1:2

**Skip If Any of These**
- Pullback retraces more than 60–70% of the entire breakout move
- Price re-enters the consolidation zone instead of holding above or below it
- Original breakout bars were weak or overlapping
- Dojis are not compressed vs the breakout bars
- Round number directly in the 60-pip path between entry and target
- Clustering bars from inside the old range block the path ahead

## ③ Range Break

**Market Context**
Price is bouncing between two visible levels — a clear top barrier and bottom barrier. Each barrier is validated by at least 2 touches with equal or near-equal highs/lows. Identify the cleaner, more streamlined barrier — that is the break side to watch. Read overall pressure — lower highs forming means bearish pressure, higher lows forming means bullish pressure.

**Setup Rules**
- At least 2 matching highs (long break) or 2 matching lows (short break) form the barrier
- Pre-breakout squeeze is present — price coiling tightly against the barrier for several bars, originating from the middle or opposite side of the range
- Overall pressure aligns with the break direction
- The barrier is clean and not debatable
- 60-pip path outside the range must be clear — no major level blocking it
- No round number within 10 pips outside the barrier in the trade direction

**Signal Bar & Entry**
- Draw barrier line across the cleanest matching highs or lows
- Wait for the full squeeze — price sandwiched against the barrier for multiple bars
- Fire market order the moment price breaks 1 pip beyond the barrier extreme — no anticipation
- If a tease break occurs and price pulls back — wait for a proper squeeze to re-form, then trade the next break as normal

**Stop & Target**
- Stop = 30 pips from entry (maximum) — back inside the range
- Target = 60 pips from entry (maximum)
- Ratio = always 1:2 minimum
- If market structure offers a tighter logical stop — use it and scale target down proportionally keeping 1:2
- Never widen the stop beyond 30 pips. Never take a target below 1:2

**Skip If Any of These**
- No squeeze — price just drifted to the barrier with no coiling
- False break — price moved straight from the opposite extreme and immediately broke with zero buildup
- Tease break — some buildup exists but not a proper squeeze — skip or wait for re-attempt
- Barrier is not clean or is debatable
- Overall pressure opposes the break direction
- Multiple false breaks already on the same side of the range
- Round number within 10 pips outside the barrier in the trade direction
- Bars printing significantly faster than normal — news release imminent

---

**Universal Rule — Applies to All Three Setups**
Never anticipate the break. Always wait for the 1-pip trigger. Stop is maximum 30 pips, target is maximum 60 pips, ratio never goes below 1:2. If the structure allows a tighter stop — take it and reduce target proportionally. A loss is simply a cost of doing business — accept it and move on. When in doubt about setup quality, stay out. You do not need to trade every setup.
